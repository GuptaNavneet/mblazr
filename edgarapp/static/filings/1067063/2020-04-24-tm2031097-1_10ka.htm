Compensation Risk Assessment

\n\n

 

\n\n

In setting compensation, the Compensation\nCommittee considers the risks to our stockholders and to achievement of our goals that may be inherent in our compensation programs.\nAt the direction of the Compensation Committee, we conducted a risk assessment of our compensation programs, including our executive\ncompensation programs. The Compensation Committee reviewed and discussed the findings of this assessment and concluded that our\ncompensation programs are designed with the appropriate balance of risk and reward in relation to our overall business strategy\nand do not incent employees to take unnecessary or excessive risks. Although a significant portion of our executive’s compensation\nis performance-based and “at-risk,” we believe our executive compensation plans are appropriately structured and are\nnot reasonably likely to result in a material adverse effect on the Company. We considered the following elements of our executive\ncompensation plans and policies when evaluating whether such plans and policies encourage our executives to take unreasonable risks:

\n\n

 

\n\n\n
·We set performance criteria that we believe are reasonable in light of past performance and market conditions, and we use a\nvariety of performance metrics that we believe correlate to long-term creation of stockholder value and that are affected by management\ndecisions;
\n\n

 

\n\n\n
·Our executive compensation program includes an appropriate balance of fixed versus variable pay, cash versus equity, and short-term\nversus long-term incentive compensation elements.
\n\n

 

\n\n\n
·We provide a significant portion of long-term incentive compensation in the form of LTIP Units. The amounts ultimately earned\nunder the awards are tied to how we perform over a three-year measurement period based on attainment of absolute and relative TSR\nperformance, and a portion of the LTIP Units are subject to additional years of service vesting, which focuses management on sustaining\nour long-term performance;
\n\n

 

\n\n\n
·Assuming achievement of at least a minimum level of performance, payouts under our performance-based awards have a range of\npayout opportunity and may result in some compensation at levels below full target achievement, rather than an “all-or-nothing”\napproach; and
\n\n

 

\n\n\n
·The Compensation Committee considers non-financial and other qualitative performance factors in determining actual compensation\npayouts.
\n\n

 

\n\n

In sum, we believe our executive compensation program is structured\nso that (i) we maintain a conservative risk profile that and aims to achieve strong stockholder returns and long-term results;\n(ii) we avoid the type of disproportionately large short-term incentives that could encourage executives to take risks that\nmay not be in our long-term interests; (iii) we provide incentives to manage for long-term performance; and (iv) a considerable\namount of wealth of our executives is tied to our long-term success. We believe this combination of factors encourages our executives\nto manage the Company in a prudent manner. The Compensation Committee specifically considered compensation risk implications during\nits deliberations on annual cash incentive plan awards and performance metrics for all executive officers.

This policy applies to any transaction, arrangement or relationship (including any indebtedness or guarantee of indebtedness),\nor any series of similar transactions, arrangements or relationships, in which (i) the Company is a participant and (ii) any “related\nperson” (defined as an employee, director, director nominee, an executive officer or someone who owns more than 5% of our\ncommon shares, or an immediate family member of any of the foregoing persons, with certain exceptions) has or will have a direct\nor indirect interest. Under the policy, the Company’s General Counsel will determine whether a transaction meets the definition\nof a related person transaction that will require review by the Nominating and Corporate Governance Committee. The Nominating and\nCorporate Governance Committee will review all related person transactions referred to them and, based on the relevant facts and\ncircumstances, will decide whether or not to approve such transactions. Only those transactions that are in, or are not inconsistent\nwith, the best interests of the Company and its stockholders will be approved. If the Company becomes aware of an existing related\nperson transaction that was not approved under this policy, the matter will be referred to the Nominating and Corporate Governance\nCommittee and it will evaluate all options available, including ratification, amendment or termination of the transaction.

\n\n

 

\n\n

The Company has determined that, under the\npolicy, the following types of transactions will be deemed to be pre-approved: (i) employment of an executive officer if the related\ncompensation is required to be reported in the Company’s proxy statement; (ii) employment of an executive officer if he or\nshe is not an immediate family member of another executive officer or director of the Company, the related compensation would have\nbeen reported in the Company’s proxy statement if he or she was a “named executive officer” and the Company’s\nExecutive Compensation and Option Committee approved (or recommended that the Board approve) such compensation; (iii) compensation\npaid to a director if the compensation is required to be reported in the Company’s proxy statement; (iv) any transaction\nwhere the related person’s interest arises solely from the ownership of the Common Stock and all holders of the Common Stock\nreceived the same benefit on a pro rata basis; (v) any transaction in which the rates or charges incurred are subject to\ngovernmental regulation; and (vi) any transaction involving bank depositary of funds, transfer agent, registrar, trustee under\na trust indenture or similar services.
